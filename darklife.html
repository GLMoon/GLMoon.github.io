Darklife

9/10

Make Plunderphonics Interesting Again

Plunderphonics as a concept is very interesting and full of untapped potential. Theoretically, an artist can treat an entire song as an instrument to leverage the familiarity and cultural impact of iconic tunes. In reality though, it's been reduced to basic sample flipping. Especially in Vaporwave with future funk, and the iconic project "Floral Shoppe," the level of sample manipulation is so basic and bare the exciting potential seemed all but snuffed out. It's been severely limited by the imagination of the artists, and the A E S T H E T I C.

Darklife is an exception, and a very welcome glimpse of that exciting potential I once saw in plunderphonics. DDS have always been adept in transfiguring and melting any samples into their works to create their signature, glitchy sound, but until Darklife, it lacked one critical element that made it incomplete: A catchy, coherent structure to make it more accessible. I'll Try Living Like This definitely demonstrated what DDS is capable of, but many songs in the project are simply not accessible enough and it hinders the full-length experience. Darklife sounds more hyperpop than vaporwave; it's catchy, familiar structured songs made with unfamiliar sounds.

AOTY for 2022.


Favourite Tracks: Messe de E-102, Rare Angel, Dark Matter
