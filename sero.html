Serotonin II

8/10

Where it's headed

This project is the closest thing to what I envision to be the next big thing. A glitchy, digital pop with hyperreal aesthetics.

Immediately from the cover, I'm induced into a hyperreal dimension. Am I staring at the artist? Is this an Avatar? A Doll? I can't tell what's real.

Throughout this project, Yeule's voice is drenched in effects, and often scattered sparse in the songs acting more like another instrument. After hearing the entire project multiple times, I can't really tell what Yeule's voice sounds like. Again, I can't tell what's real.

The music itself though, now that's the real fucking thing. The production is amazing, and the instrumentation especially scratches an itch for me. They're generally very accessible pop songs with an ethereal vibe created by its lush synth sounds. An Angel Held Me Like a Child is a great example of this. It's an emotional EDM track that makes even a non EDM fan like me want to dance at the beat drop. It isn't really ground breaking in its composition or anything, but much like HyperPop, it's unconventional sounds packaged in a conventional structure. I also enjoy the small tidbits of Piano sprinkled in there to ground the tracks a little bit. It's too floaty, if you know what I mean.

Music is becoming more digital, and the world more Hyperreal. The distinction between what we consider real is so varied and loose the idea of an objective reality sounds almost laughable to me. This type of music embodies that aesthetic, and for that I think it's only going to get more popular.


Favourite Tracks: An Angel Held Me Like a Child
